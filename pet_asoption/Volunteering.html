Becoming a Volunteer

To volunteer with Tabby Cats Rescue, you must fulfil the following conditions:

  • 1. Over 15 years of age.

  • 2. If you are under 18 years of age we will require permission to volunteer with us from a parent/guardian.


If you are interested in a college / school work placement with us, please email us on tabbycatsrescue@gmail.com.



We have a few different roles available, including:

  • More details:

  • Events Organiser

    As a voluntary events organiser you’ll play a key role in organising and running our awareness and fundraising events. Working in conjunction with our Shelter management team, you’ll be involved in the planning of our events as well as ensuring that everything runs as smoothly as possible. This voluntary role will suit a person who has helped organise events before, or who is studying for an events management career. For more details, please email us.



    Events Volunteer

    As an events volunteer you’ll assist an events organiser at our exciting events across Yorkshire throughout the year. 2024 marks the 20th anniversary of the Tabby Cats Rescue, and we’ll be celebrating in style. Our events will provide a great opportunity for us to spread the message about our vital work as well as contributing to our fundraising efforts. This role is perfect for enthusiastic volunteers who love cats, love events and love meeting people. Experience is great, but enthusiasm is even more important!

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What’s in it for the volunteer?

    It’s a great way to meet like-minded people who share your enthusiasm for animal welfare. You will have the opportunity to gain new skills and develop existing ones, whilst making a real difference to the lives of animals and their humans.

  • Any more details?

    For more information on becoming a Charity Trustee, and to ensure it’s the right decision for you, we recommend visiting: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charity-trustee-whats-involved

  • What will you be doing?

    Trustees are volunteers who use their skills to direct how a charity is managed and run, to enable it to meet its charitable purpose. Our trustees lend their professional skills to our charity to help us work towards our goal of relieving and preventing the suffering and distress amongst cats in need of care and attention and to promote the welfare of cats. The core trustee role is outlined below.
    • To be familiar with and support the aims and policies of the shelter.
    • To work in conjunction with your fellow trustees and the charity to jointly act in making decisions for the best interest of the charity.
    • To actively participate in regular board meetings every 6 weeks and to attend the annual general meeting.
    • To act at all times as a good ambassador for the charity.

  • Time commitment

    • For the role of trustee, the time commitment is 2 hours every 6 weeks to attend board meetings at the Tabby Cats Rescue. Our board meetings usually take place on a Tuesday evening 6pm-8pm, but this is flexible and open to rearrangement to suit the trustees.
    • Outside of the commitments listed above, it is entirely up to you how much time you volunteer.
